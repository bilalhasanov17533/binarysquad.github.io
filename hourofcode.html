Hour of code

A brief report about the event we hosted

How was it planned?

Our initial step was planning an appropriate meeting with students. Our team planned the event through our WhatsApp group and met in person for discussions. Kanan printed the certificates, and we were ready to go. We decided on Baku IB School Lyceum No.6 as the event location and agreed to host it on November 2nd. The Hour of Code event was held with 20 6thgrade students at that school. For the sake of being close to their current curricular activity, we hosted Hour of Code during their IT subject. To begin, we introduced students to our educational institution, ADA university and its faculties. We talked about our multinational group which is open to all nations all over the world.

What challenges have we faced?

There were a couple of students who constantly asked questions, demonstrated good knowledge, and were eager to learn more. We got such students to volunteer in taking part in the real process of writing code for a specific task by combining the interactive blocks supported in our program. Initially, the website language was in English, and Azerbaijani sector students had trouble understanding tasks, so Bilal considered changing the language to make the tasks clear for students. An additional act in the process was that Turgay made some fake and wrong situations to demonstrate that computers are very precise about small mistakes, and they must understand problems and steps to solution and output down to the minute detail. The point was that one wrong statement may crush the whole structure of the code. Regarding this point, two actively participating students added comments and brought up specific examples from Minecraft

Q&A with Students

We had a Q&A time with students about the usage of coding in diverse areas. We answered the questions, and summarized by saying that coding stays behind the system they pay for their things, the games they play, the videos and images they see on different social platforms, and their family records at their school. We told the students that there are a tremendous number of resources in the online world when it comes to coding, problem-solving, and computational skills in case they want to enhance their knowledge, skills, and information in these fields.

And Finally...

We would like to thank the school staff for welcoming us with open arms, as well as the students who participated and actively showed interest. We wish the best of luck to future Hour of Code event makers in their intriguing and insightful journey.
