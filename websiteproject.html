Our Website Project

How did it start?

When we were given a limited time to complete this assignment
and briefly learn 3 languages, we were slighltly worried. Knowing
that we had other classes and assignments, we immediately started
taking online courses from Codeacademy that was provided to us,
and most of us finished it within a week. We were suprised with
how quickly you can learn HTML and CSS, although not fully, but
know enough to be able to construct a simple website. Considering
the fact that Codeacademy is free and for everyone, we did not face
any difficulties learning their courses. Eitherway, as simple as it
sounds, we faced quite a number of challenges during the process of
building this website.

What challenges did we face?

First and foremost, Bilal had a lot of assignments due in a short time
period, as well as an upcoming physics exam shortly after the Homework
was due. So we decided to discuss things in advance. We started by
coming up with a team name, which would turn out to be BinarySquad.
We chose this name because it represents 4 members (hence the name,
"squad") and what we are passionate about (no, not binary numbers, but
computer science and/or IT). Since we all had different schedules,
gathering together was a major setback which would delay our work. We
eventually found time to gather together and work on the website a few
times, however, as time went by, it gradually became harder for all four
of us to get together in real life. Instead, we split up into pairs, and
worked on specific sections of the website, and then once we found time,
we met to further enhance those sections. Additionally, our github was a
mess. A lot of files were placed into the team folder with similar names
, which was confusing and frustrating to organize. We managed to
complete our website just before the deadline, which although risky,
felt rewarding.

What else?

In conclusion, we are happy with the result. We tried our best to keep
the design minimalistic and professional. Communicating with eachother
was a key element when it came to making things work. This website
was created using HTML, CSS, and a bit of JavaScript. Codeacademy
came to great help, and we would encourage everybody to use it. You
can find our Codeacademy profiles here: Bilal, Turgay, Kanan, Valid.

P.S: All the images that you see on this website belong to us,
and were taken within ADA university. Oh and, this website is
NOT supported for mobile devices.
